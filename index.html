Why I Build

It all started from one simple need — a chat app that is free and fully mine.

My work is defined not by titles or years of experience, but by what I actually build. Every project here began as a genuine personal need — and was turned into something real, functional, and built to last.

Chatme was born not because I wanted to learn Flutter. It was born because I needed a chat application completely free from third-party servers and subscriptions — one I fully own and control. That single need led me down a deep rabbit hole: the Nostr decentralized protocol, NIP-04 encrypted messaging, and the secp256k1 elliptic curve powering it all.

Curiosity didn't stop at usage. I started asking: how does this curve actually work mathematically? What are its parameters? Why these specific values?

I studied the fundamentals of elliptic curve cryptography — not from a textbook, but through implementation. I built things, broke them, studied the results, and rebuilt. Through that process of methodical experimentation, a new and ambitious question emerged: what if I designed my own curve from scratch?

That question became Curve256189 — a fully custom elliptic curve cryptography library, complete with its own scalar protection layer. Not borrowed. Not inherited. Built from the ground up, driven entirely by curiosity and personal necessity.

I approach every problem with a simple philosophy: understand it by building it. Not a senior developer. Not a mathematician. But a builder who is deeply invested in the craft — and in doing things properly.

Curve256189

Active Research
Dart Cryptography ECC SageMath

A custom elliptic curve cryptography library built from scratch in Dart. Features FPOW (Fixed-Point One-Way Wrap) — a hash-based scalar protection layer designed as a quantum hardening mechanism, formally verified via SageMath. Long-term goal: to serve as the cryptographic backbone of Chatme, fully independent of the Nostr ecosystem.

// Motivation

Began with curiosity about secp256k1 in Nostr. Spent months studying ECC fundamentals through hands-on implementation. Eventually, one question changed everything: what if I designed my own curve? 68 test cases across 9 attack categories. A long journey — fully enjoyed.
